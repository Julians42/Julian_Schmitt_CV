Seismology Research Position

March 2020 - Present

I am working in Julia and on AWS to help create an updated model of expected peak ground velocities during a future LA area earthquake using M. Denolle’s ambient noise cross-correlation method. The project, called BASIN is focused on improving ground motion predictions in the Los Angeles area via both empirical (my project) and numerical modeling approaches (others at SDSU, Caltech, and Stanford). See Research section for more details.

Case Analyst for Harvard CBE

Spring 2019

In the spring of 2019 I worked on a Harvard Undergraduate Consulting for Business and the Environment case team developing a food waste management protocol for a large non-profit. Personally, I was responsible for data analytics, working to save on disposal costs by developing an optimization model for subsidiaries to estimate the most cost-effective mixture of disposal methods. In addition to providing data-collection and communication advice, I designed a mock website which exemplified improved data collection methods, as well as providing additional tools subsidiaries could use to make more informed waste management decisions.

Math Modeling

Spring 2017 and 2018

My Middlebury Mathematics Team competed in both the 2017 and 2018 national Moody’s, and subsequently MathWorks, Math Modeling Challenges. In 2017, we earned a scholarship placing in the top five percent of teams while investigating the effects of climate change on the US’s five coastal National Parks. In 2018, the team investigated the issue of food waste, placing in the top 6 teams out of 913, each earning a $1000 scholarship and the opportunity to present our findings on an all-expenses paid trip to Jane Street Financial in New York City.
