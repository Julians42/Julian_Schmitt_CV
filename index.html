Personal Profile

With a foundation in pure mathematics, statistics, and computer science, I envision myself using these skills to pursue research in earth science as I am passionate about the outdoors. This summer I am researching virtual earthquakes under Marine Denolle in the Harvard Seismology group. Outside of academics I ski on the Harvard Varsity Nordic ski team and am a somewhat accomplished trumpet player.

Experience

Seismology Research Position

March 2020 - Present

I am working in Julia and on AWS to help create an updated model of expected peak ground velocities during a future LA area earthquake using M. Denolle’s ambient noise cross-correlation method. The project, called BASIN is focused on improving ground motion predictions in the Los Angeles area via both empirical (my project) and numerical modeling approaches (others at SDSU, Caltech, and Stanford).

Case Analyst for Harvard CBE

Spring 2019

In the spring of 2019 I worked on a Harvard Undergraduate Consulting for Business and the Environment case team developing a food waste management protocol for a large non-profit. Personally, I was responsible for data analytics, working to save on disposal costs by developing an optimization model for subsidiaries to estimate the most cost-effective mixture of disposal methods. In addition to providing data-collection and communication advice, I designed a mock website which exemplified improved data collection methods, as well as providing additional tools subsidiaries could use to make more informed waste management decisions.
